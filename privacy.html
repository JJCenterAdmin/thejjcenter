Section 1

Who We Are

The Jehovah Jireh Community Development Center Inc. ("The JJ Center," "we," "us," or "our") is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization located in Ward 8, Washington, DC, founded in 2005. Our mission is to serve our community through free food distributions and health education programs grounded in the principle of "food as medicine."

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our website at jjcenter.org, our member portal, our mobile application, or interact with us in person at our food pantry events.

Questions about this policy?

Contact our Privacy Team at hello@jjcenter.org. All privacy requests are handled by our operations team and reviewed by JJ Center leadership.

Section 2

What Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

  • Account registration: First name, last name, email address, and password when you create a member account.
  • Walk-in pantry visits: Name, ZIP code, household size, and optionally email address or phone number when you check in at our welcome desk.
  • Health program opt-in (voluntary only): Health topics of interest and any health-related information you choose to share with our health professionals. This information is entirely optional and is never required to use our services or receive food assistance.
  • Communications: Messages or feedback you send to us.

Information Collected Automatically

  • Visit logs: Date and time of each pantry visit when you check in using our QR system.
  • Device information: Browser type, operating system, and IP address for security purposes.
  • Usage data: Pages viewed and features used within your member dashboard.

Information We Do NOT Collect

  • Social Security numbers or government ID numbers
  • Financial information or payment data
  • Immigration status
  • Race, ethnicity, or national origin (unless voluntarily shared for program purposes)
🔒 HIPAA Notice — Health Information

Any health-related information you share is collected under your explicit consent only, is governed by HIPAA's Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 164), and is handled with the highest level of protection. You may withdraw consent at any time without affecting your access to our food pantry or other services.

Section 3

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect only to:

  • Operate and maintain your member account and dashboard
  • Log and display your pantry visit history
  • Send notifications about pantry events, schedule changes, and program updates (only if you opt in)
  • Deliver personalized health education content and videos from our medical professionals (only if you opt into the health program)
  • Post community announcements from JJ Center leadership to your dashboard feed
  • Measure and report our community impact using anonymous, aggregate statistics — individual data is never published
  • Maintain the security of our systems and prevent fraud
  • Comply with applicable federal and DC laws
  • Support grant reporting requirements (anonymous aggregate data only)

We will never use your information to discriminate against you, sell advertising, or share data with any party for commercial purposes.

Section 4

Protected Health Information (HIPAA)

The JJ Center operates in compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) and its implementing regulations, including the Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 164) and the Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C).

What Is Protected Health Information (PHI)

PHI is any information that both (a) relates to your health condition, treatment, or payment, and (b) could be used to identify you. At the JJ Center, PHI is only created when you voluntarily opt into our health program and share health-related information in combination with your identity.

How We Handle PHI

  • PHI is stored in a separate, encrypted database table with access restricted to authorized health professionals and administrators only
  • All staff and volunteers who may access PHI have signed HIPAA confidentiality agreements
  • PHI is never shared with any third party without your explicit written consent, except as required by law
  • PHI is retained for a minimum of 6 years from the date of creation, as required by HIPAA
  • You have the right to access, amend, and request deletion of your PHI at any time

Breach Notification

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay, and no later than 60 calendar days from the discovery of the breach. Breaches affecting 500 or more individuals will also be reported to the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services (HHS) Office for Civil Rights.

⚠️ Your Health Opt-In Is Always Voluntary

Opting into the health program is completely voluntary. Choosing not to participate will never affect your ability to receive food assistance or any other JJ Center services. You may withdraw from the health program at any time by visiting your dashboard or contacting us at hello@jjcenter.org.

Section 5

How We Protect Your Data (NIST)

The JJ Center implements security controls aligned with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) and NIST Special Publication 800-53 (Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems). Our security program includes:

Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption at rest: All data stored using AES-256 encryption via Supabase (AWS infrastructure)
  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted using TLS 1.3
  • Access control: Role-based access control (RBAC) — members see only their own data; supervisors see only what their role requires
  • Authentication: Secure password requirements, email verification, and optional two-factor authentication (2FA) via SMS
  • Session management: Automatic session timeout after 30 minutes of inactivity
  • Audit logging: All access to sensitive data is logged with timestamp, user identity, and action performed

Administrative Safeguards

  • HIPAA training required for all staff and volunteers before accessing any member data
  • Annual security review conducted each June
  • Minimum necessary access principle — personnel only access data required for their specific role
  • Immediate access revocation upon staff or volunteer departure

Physical Safeguards

  • No sensitive member data stored on physical devices or paper records
  • All data is cloud-hosted with Supabase (SOC 2 Type II certified infrastructure)
  • Welcome desk tablets are secured and require login before use
ℹ️ NIST Framework Alignment

Our controls map to the NIST CSF functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, and Recover. A full technical safeguards checklist is maintained internally and reviewed annually by the System Administrator and CEO.

Section 6

Email Communications (CAN-SPAM)

The JJ Center complies with the CAN-SPAM Act of 2003 (15 U.S.C. § 7701 et seq.). All commercial and promotional emails we send will:

  • Clearly identify The JJ Center, Inc. as the sender
  • Include our organization name: The Jehovah Jireh Community Development Center Inc.
  • Include a clear and conspicuous unsubscribe mechanism in every email
  • Honor all unsubscribe requests within 10 business days
  • Never use deceptive subject lines or sender information

Types of Emails We Send

  • Transactional: Account confirmation, password reset, visit confirmation — these are required for account operation and cannot be unsubscribed from while your account is active
  • Pantry notifications: Event reminders and schedule changes — opt in/out in your dashboard at any time
  • Health content: Personalized health tips and video recommendations — only if you opt into the health program
  • Announcements: Updates from JJ Center leadership — only sent to active member accounts
  • Walk-in invitation: A one-time account creation invitation sent after your first pantry visit if you provide your email — you may decline with no consequence

Unsubscribe

You may unsubscribe from any non-transactional emails at any time by:

  • Clicking the "Unsubscribe" link at the bottom of any email
  • Updating your notification preferences in your member dashboard
  • Emailing us at hello@jjcenter.org with "Unsubscribe" in the subject line
Section 7

Third Parties & Data Sharing

The JJ Center does not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing or commercial purposes — ever.

Service Providers

We use the following service providers to operate our platform. All are bound by confidentiality agreements and may only use your data to perform services for us:

  • Supabase (database and authentication) — U.S.-based, SOC 2 certified; a HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is in place
  • Vercel (website hosting) — U.S.-based; no user data is stored
  • Google Workspace (email and operations) — a HIPAA BAA is in place; PHI is never sent via email

Required Disclosures

We may disclose your information only when required by law, including:

  • In response to a valid subpoena, court order, or government request
  • To report a HIPAA breach to HHS as required by federal law
  • To protect the safety of any person in an emergency situation

Section 9

Children's Privacy

Our member portal and mobile application are intended for individuals 13 years of age and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent, in compliance with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).

Individuals under 18 may use our food pantry services and have an account created by a parent or guardian. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately at hello@jjcenter.org and we will delete the information promptly.

Section 10

Data Retention

  • Account data (name, email): Retained until you delete your account
  • Visit history: Retained for 3 years for program reporting purposes
  • Protected Health Information (PHI): Retained for a minimum of 6 years from the date of creation, as required by HIPAA
  • Walk-in records (no account): Retained for 2 years for impact reporting, then permanently deleted
  • Security and audit logs: Retained for 6 years
  • HIPAA confidentiality agreements: Retained for duration of service plus 3 years

When data is deleted, it is permanently and irreversibly removed from all systems, including backups, within 90 days.

Section 11

Mobile App

The JJ Center mobile application (currently in development) will use the same Supabase backend and apply identical privacy protections as described in this policy. The app will:

  • Request only the permissions necessary for its features (camera for QR scanning, notifications if you opt in)
  • Never access your contacts, location, microphone, or photos without explicit permission
  • Operate under the same session timeout and 2FA protections as the web portal
  • Be available on iOS (App Store) and Android (Google Play), both of which require and have reviewed this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy URL (jjcenter.org/privacy.html) is the policy submitted to Apple App Store and Google Play Store for app review and approval.

Section 12

Changes to This Policy

We review and update this Privacy Policy annually each June, or sooner if required by changes in law or our data practices. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page
  • Send a notification to all active member accounts via email
  • Post a notice in the member dashboard for 30 days

Your continued use of our services after the effective date of a revised policy constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy. If you do not agree with the changes, you may delete your account at any time.
