The Crisis

This is not food insecurity.
This is food apartheid.

Ward 8 is home to nearly 75,000 Washingtonians — but has access to just one full-service grocery store and no regular farmers' markets. Residents are forced to survive on processed corner-store goods, driving disproportionately high rates of diabetes, obesity, and hypertension.

This is not an accident. It is a systemic failure. And it requires a clinical response.

Our Solution

A Clinically Directed Food Pantry

We cross the geographic divide — partnering with grocery stores across the DMV to source fresh produce, lean proteins, and nutrient-dense foods, bringing them directly to Ward 8 families who need them most. Every week. No barriers.

🥦

Radical Accessibility

Set up outdoors at Oxon Run Park every week in a farmers' market style. Fresh, healthy food completely free. No eligibility requirements, no limits on visits, no one ever turned away.

🩺

Clinical Oversight

Food inventory curated by MDs, PharmDs, Licensed Nutritionists, and Public Health experts — specifically selected to combat chronic disease in our community.

🏫

Wellness Workshops

On-site Healthy Living workshops teaching medical nutrition, chronic disease management, and preventative health. Education alongside every distribution.

🚚

DMV Grocery Network

We actively source fresh, healthy food from grocery store partners across DC, Maryland, and Virginia — and bring it directly back to Ward 8 families every single week.

Leadership

Dr. Mary A. Roach, PharmD

CEO & Founder of The JJ Center, Inc., Dr. Roach is a licensed pharmacist with a Doctor of Pharmacy degree — and a lifelong advocate for the health and dignity of Ward 8 residents.

She founded The JJ Center on a conviction that food insecurity is not a resource problem — it is a public health crisis requiring a clinical response. Under her leadership, the organization has secured over $1 million in scholarships for youth, spearheaded food access initiatives, and is now building a full community transformation ecosystem through the Cornerstone Care Coalition (CCC).

The Bigger Story

Beyond the Pantry —
The Cornerstone Care Coalition

The food pantry is our anchor — but The JJ Center's mission reaches further. Through the Cornerstone Care Coalition (CCC), we are building a full community transformation ecosystem across Ward 7 and Ward 8 — addressing the Social Determinants of Health that drive inequality in our community.

The vision: a community where every resident has access to food, health, housing, economic opportunity, and support — and a blueprint that can be replicated nationally.

🧠Behavioral health & mental wellness
🏠Housing stability & homelessness prevention
💼Workforce development & AI certifications
💰Financial literacy & homeownership pathways
🏥Chronic disease management & CCBHC clinic (coming)
🤝Small business & entrepreneurship support
